About Karnataka Real Estate Regulatory Authority

The Real Estate Regulation and Development (RERA) Act, 2016 is considered as one of the landmark legislations passed by the Government of India. Its objective is to reform the real estate sector in India, encouraging greater transparency, citizen centricity, accountability and financial discipline. This is in line with the vast and growing economy of India as in future many people will be investing in real estate sector.

In Karnataka, RERA has played a pivotal role in ensuring that home buyers are protected and developers remain accountable. Through strict compliance, project registration, and timely updates, the Karnataka Real Estate Regulatory Authority has contributed significantly to sustainable urban development and consumer confidence in the real estate market.

Real Estate Regulatory Authority (RERA) was established under the Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 to bring transparency, accountability, and efficiency to the real estate sector. Karnataka RERA (KRERA) was officially established on 24th July 2017 to regulate real estate projects and agents within the state.

About RERA Act

Welcome to the official portal of Real Estate Regulatory Authority Karnataka. This application has been developed for the benefit of all the stakeholders of the real estate industry more particularly the general public and consumers. This website has been developed under Sec 4(3) of the Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act 2016.

The Department of Housing, Government of Karnataka is the nodal department for implementation of the Act in the State of Karnataka. The Act came into force w.e.f 1st May 2017 and the Rules have been notified by the Government of Karnataka on 10th July 2017.

Since its implementation, Karnataka RERA has registered thousands of projects and agents, resolved consumer disputes, and introduced digital platforms for faster grievance redressal. It continues to focus on transparency, financial discipline, and promoting trust between developers and buyers, thereby boosting real estate growth in the state.
